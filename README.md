## PROJECT OVERVIEW

Addie LaRue exists but no one remembers her. She walks through the world like breath on glass, leaving no imprint, no record, no lasting trace. This interactive narrative experience invites the player to enter her story across five cinematic acts: from the life she fled in 1691 Villon, through the night she made a deal with the darkness, into three centuries of invisibility, and finally to a moment of impossible choice.

This is not a game about winning. It is an experience about bearing witness. About the difference between being seen and being recorded. About the fragile, conditional nature of memory, and the choice to remember someone even when the system insists she was never there.

*So, player — what will be your fate? Will you remain invisible, or will you create a mark for yourself?*

## THEMATIC STRANDS

The experience is built around six core literary strands, each explored through specific interactive and visual elements:

**Character**
Addie is constructed through her relationships and through the player's choices. The dialogue system allows the player to shape how Addie responds to Luc, and the museum artifacts reflect the emotional arc of those choices. The wooden ring, the leather jacket, the seven freckles: each object is an argument about who she was.

**Setting**
The marsh near Villon, the Parisian streets, the New Orleans apartment, the Brooklyn bookshop. Settings are rendered as atmosphere rather than geography, conveyed through background color shifts, vignette changes, and layered lighting. The environment reflects emotional states rather than literal locations.

**Symbolism**
Every artifact in the Memory Museum carries layered symbolic weight. The wooden ring is not just carved ash; it is the cost of freedom made physical. The polaroids are not just failed photographs; they are evidence of erasure. The onyx watch is not just a timepiece; it is the countdown of a soul.

**Structure**
The narrative is told in fragmented, nonlinear fashion, jumping between Addie's origin, her curse, and moments of dialogue with Luc. The Memory Museum reorganizes these fragments into a coherent argument about her existence, mirroring the way Owens structures *Where the Crawdads Sing* with alternating timeline chapters.

**Theme**
Abandonment, isolation, the cost of freedom, love as witness versus possession, and the central question: If no one remembers you, did you ever really live? The search archive at the end makes this literal: the database either confirms her existence, or it wavers.

**Point of View**
The player becomes a co-creator of Addie's memory. The clinical voice of the search system ("No record exists") contrasts with the personal, invested voice of the museum labels. Institutional memory claims neutrality. The museum argues otherwise.

## HOW TO PLAY

**Step 1 — Advance the Story**
Click anywhere or press any key. The narrative unfolds line by line. Poetic fragments, dialogue, and internal thoughts appear one at a time. You control the pace. There is no timer. There is no rush.

**Step 2 — Find the Hidden Fragments**
Throughout Acts I through IV, small objects flicker at the edges of the screen: a folded letter, a moth circling, a bird feather, a pressed flower, a candle stub. Click them before they fade. Each one you collect unlocks a corresponding artifact in the Memory Museum. Not every memory announces itself. Some must be found.

**Step 3 — Choose Your Response**
In Act III, the darkness addresses you directly. You will be given three ways to respond: Resistance, Reflection, or Surrender. There is no wrong answer. Your choice does not change what happens, but it changes what it means. Luc will remember how you answered. And so will the archive.

**Step 4 — Search the Archive**
At the end, a search bar appears. Type the name "Addie LaRue" and press enter. The database will return a result. What it says depends on the choices you made. If you showed openness, the record is restored. If you showed only resistance, the record wavers.

**Step 5 — Enter the Memory Museum**
After the search, a link appears. Click "Enter the Memory Museum" to explore the artifacts you have unlocked. Each object carries a label explaining its significance. Locked artifacts appear grayed out, waiting to be discovered on a second journey.

## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

The experience is divided into five acts, plus an epilogue and museum:

**Act I — The Life She Escapes**
1691. Villon, France. A betrothal arranged before Addie could speak her own desires. Her father carved her a wooden ring from ash. Estele, the old woman, taught her to pray to the gods that come after dark. The wedding date drew closer. And then she ran.

**Act II — The Night She Runs**
The night swallowed her whole. Wind. Darkness. A presence. Luc offers her a deal: freedom at a cost she will not understand until it is too late. She accepts without full knowledge.

**Act III — The Curse**
At first, she did not notice. Then a shopkeeper forgot her face. A friend had blank eyes. She left no imprint, no trace, no memory. Two dialogue moments with Luc punctuate this act, giving the player agency over Addie's emotional stance.

**Act IV — The Possibility**
Luc returns with a new offer: a temporary restoration of memory. For a brief moment, Addie can be remembered. Her name is spoken. And this time, it stays.

**Act V — The Contradiction**
Immediately after the emotional resolution, the experience cuts to a clinical reality interface. Courier Prime font. Parchment background. A single line: "No record exists for: Addie LaRue." The system denies what the player just witnessed.

**Epilogue — Search Archive**
"Who are you?" A search bar appears. The player types her name. The database returns a result conditioned on the choices made throughout Act III.

**Memory Museum**
A collection of artifacts unlocked through hidden fragments and story completion. Each artifact has a detailed curator-style label analyzing its symbolic significance.

## THE CHOICE SYSTEM

Two dialogue moments in Act III present the player with three options each:

| Option | Emotional Stance | Luc's Tone |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Resistance | Addie pushes back | Cold and distant |
| Reflection | Addie questions | Philosophical |
| Surrender | Addie accepts | Intimate and warm |

Luc's responses change tone based on the player's choice, meaning two different players experience a different emotional register of the same story.

The player's choices feed into a tracking array. At the end, the search archive returns different results based on emotional tone:

- Any Surrender or Reflection chosen: "Status: Found · Exists in memory"
- Only Resistance chosen: "Status: Unstable · Partial match"

This encodes the theme that memory is conditional, shaped by the quality of our relationships and the willingness of others to witness us.

## HIDDEN FRAGMENT SYSTEM

Five clickable objects appear during Acts I through IV. Players who observe carefully and click them collect memory fragments that unlock additional museum artifacts.

| Fragment | Appears In | Unlocks |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Folded Letter | Act I | The Blue Notebook |
| Pressed Flower | Act II | Pressed Marsh Flower |
| Moth | Acts II & III | The Black Leather Jacket |
| Bird Feather | Act III | The Fifth Bird (Revenir) |
| Candle Stub | Acts III & IV | Candle Stub |

Fragments appear with a subtle glow animation and disappear with a fade when collected. A toast notification confirms each collection. The system rewards attention to detail and encourages multiple playthroughs.

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

The experience is built as a single-page application using three files:

**index.html**
Contains all phase divs, hidden fragment elements, atmospheric layers (vignette, ink texture, atmosphere gradient), the collection toast notification, music toggle button, artifact detail overlay, and the full Memory Museum grid. Links to Google Fonts for typography.

**styles.css**
Contains all visual rules organized by section: CSS custom properties, base reset, atmosphere layers, vignette, fade overlay, phase management, narrative lines, dialogue, choice UI, resolution text, contradiction screen, hidden fragments, music toggle, search archive, memory museum, artifact detail overlay, collection toast, and responsive media queries for mobile.

**scripts.js**
Contains all interactive logic: global state management, DOM references, fade helpers, phase activation and deactivation, background and atmosphere control, hidden fragment collection system, Act I through Act V advancement and transition functions, dialogue moment handling, search archive logic with conditional results based on emotional tone, museum artifact builder and renderer, artifact detail overlay show and hide, and all event listeners.

The experience uses no external libraries or frameworks. All transitions are CSS-based. All logic is vanilla JavaScript.

**Key Technical Features:**
- CSS custom properties for consistent theming
- CSS animations for fade transitions and glitch effects
- JavaScript state machine for phase management
- Conditional rendering based on player choices
- Dynamic DOM manipulation for museum artifact generation
- Event delegation for click and keyboard interaction
- Promise-based async fade transitions
- Local state tracking for collected fragments and emotional tone

## VISUAL DESIGN

The experience follows strict cinematic design principles:

- Full-screen scene transitions with fade to black between acts
- Dark, moody gradients for narrative scenes
- Soft lighting shifts driven by CSS radial gradients
- Centered serif typography for emotional dialogue
- Minimal interface visible at any time
- No dashboards, no HUDs, no UI chrome except where thematically justified
- Slow, deliberate pacing controlled by the player
- Atmospheric layers (ink texture, vignette, ambient light) that shift with each phase

The design intent is that the player feels like they are moving through a memory, not operating software.

## TYPOGRAPHY

Three typefaces are used with specific thematic purposes:

**IM Fell English** (serif)
Used for all narrative text, dialogue, poetic lines, and artifact descriptions. An 18th-century typeface that evokes the period of Addie's origin. The italic variant is used for emotional emphasis.

**IM Fell English SC** (small caps)
Used for titles, chapter labels, Luc's dialogue, choice prompts, and museum artifact names. Provides an engraved, period-accurate feel.

**Courier Prime** (monospace)
Used exclusively in the contradiction screen and search archive. The font shift from serif to monospace encodes the thematic break from memory into system, from human into institutional record.

All fonts are loaded from Google Fonts and fall back to system serif and monospace fonts if unavailable.

## HOW TO RUN

1. Download all files into a single folder.
2. Ensure `index.html`, `styles.css`, and `scripts.js` are in the same directory.
3. If using the scrapbook frame, place `frame.png` in the same folder.
4. If using background music, place your audio file as `background-music.mp3` in the same folder.
5. Open `index.html` in any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
6. No server, build tools, or internet connection required after fonts are cached.
